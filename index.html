01 — About
Who I Am

I'm a biotechnology researcher at the Yu Chen Lab, University of South Florida, where my work sits at the intersection of structural biology, protein engineering, and computational drug discovery.

My research spans EPO protein engineering & crystallization, SARS-CoV-2 Mpro inhibitor development, and neonatal human intestinal enteroid models — translating fundamental molecular insights into therapeutic strategies.

Beyond the bench, I'm the CEO & Founder of Bio-VReality, a startup building AI-powered virtual laboratories for science education — making immersive, hands-on science accessible to every student.

I'm actively pursuing PhD opportunities in structural biology and protein biochemistry, targeting programs at institutions like Scripps, UCSF, and UW Seattle.

02 — Research
Current Projects
01

EPO Dimer Engineering

Engineering a novel EPO dimer architecture with D85N, E125Q, and F93W mutations connected by a (G4S)₃ linker, targeting 3× greater potency vs. native EPO with reduced production cost. Expressed in CHO cells via pcDNA3.1.

Protein Engineering CHO Expression Crystallography STAT5 Signaling
02

SARS-CoV-2 Mpro Inhibitors

Developing inhibitors of the SARS-CoV-2 main protease (Mpro) through structure-based drug design and computational docking. Designing cell-based assays using GFP/FRET reporter systems to validate hits.

Drug Discovery Molecular Docking GNINA ProLIF
03

Neonatal Intestinal Enteroids

Developing basolateral-out immunofluorescence staining protocols for neonatal human intestinal enteroid models in collaboration with Yu Chen's Lab, adapting existing fetal protocols with neonatal-specific modifications.

Enteroids Immunofluorescence Cell Culture Protocol Dev.
04

Computational Docking Pipeline

Automating molecular docking pose shortlisting on USF's Circe HPC cluster. Python-based pipeline using ProLIF, GNINA, MDAnalysis, and RDKit to filter 1000+ docking poses down to 30–40 high-quality candidates.

HPC / Circe Python RDKit MDAnalysis
